Fast, Cheap, and Out of Control

The lion tamer, the gardener, the roboticist, and the molerat specialist; each of these men are like a god to the respective entities they control. These four men each embody an aspect of the god of the old testament. The lion tamer embodies the wrath of God, the gardener embodies God the creator of life; the roboticist, God the creator of spirit and the molerat specialist, the curious God who watches man’s peculiar behavior.
If we are to assume these men are like God, that would make the entities they control analagous to man. Man strives to be like a God and have control. Despite this he is inevitably controlled by forces outside his comprehension. Like the lion confounded by the four legged chair, like the robots programmed to do what is in their nature; man in fact has very little control over his own destiny. To compensate for the insecurities this raises, we endeavor to control tameable entities such as molerats and trees.
